Pointing Performance of Touch Screen vs. Touch Pad

Keke Wu | Supriya Naidu

*Description*

In this study, we compared the pointing performance between Touch Screen and Touch Pad using GoFitts in a labtop setting.

We conducted a within-subject study to test each participant's performance under two conditions.

Our task was to let the user point and click on the filled circle as quickly and accurately as possible.

Participant: 2, Task Type: 2D, Number of Trials: 7

*Charts*

*Summary*

In our test, Keke started with touch screen, while Supriya started with touch pad for counterbalancing purpose.

Our study shows that the user generally performs better on the touch pad than on the touch screen.

It takes less time and yields a lower error rate on the touch pad than on the touch screen.
